Homemade LED Switch - Teamwork

Team: Jueun Jeon, Sunwoo Park, Yuri Kim
  1. Brainstorming

    2. Before starting the project, we decided to submit two ideas each.

    1: night view of Manhattan
    2: Coin laundry
    3: New York Subway

    4: DDR
    5: Magician on magic circle
    6: Ducks' wedding day

    We chose Idea #1, as it requires few components, yet has a beautiful result.
  2. Ideation
    • LED lights represent the actual lights of the citys
    • Coin symbolizes how money operates the electricity

    When we first got the assignment to make a homemade switch with LED lights, the night view of Manhattan's night view came to mind. We came up with the concept of 'paying 50 cents to see Manhattan's at night view'. Then we decided to use LED lights to make the skylineManhattan's night view and to use a coin as a conductor. The coin would be used as a switch to turn on the light.

  3. Components

    4. Coin, 9V battery, 220Ω resistor, LED lights, black paper, aluminum foil, and illustration of Manhattan buildings.

  4. Illustration

    5. Before we proceeded withon making the switch, we drew an illustration of the Manhattan landscape that would become our base for this project.

  5. Schematic (circuit diagram)
    6. First Schematic

    First, we drew a schematic in which 8 LED lights are connected in a series. Following this schematic, we assembled the components. However, the lights did not turn on. We assumed that itthis wasis due to the high voltage that the eight lights were not working.

    Revised schematic

    To solve the problem in the first schematic, we tried to divide the 8 LED lights into two sectionsparts of 4 LED lights each and connected them to the 9V battery.

  6. Attempts
    7. First Attempt

    First, we tested our coin switches and made them work with our tools.

    Second attempt

    Next, we attempted it with a parallel connection instead of a serial connection. We didn’t consider the switch while assembling it. So even though the LED lamps were operating, the link wasn’t suitable for a homemade switch.

    Third attempt

    We connected the LED lights incorrectly with regard to regarding the plus(anode) and minus sides(cathode), which resulted in the lights not working. What we first attempted with the LED lights was + - - + + - - + …. . However, we had misunderstood the current flow.     Fourth attempt

    We tried a serial connection, but the power was not strong enough to make more than 4 LED lights work. Out of 8 LED lights in a row, only 4 seemed to be fully working. This is why we came up with a revised schematic system and returned to the combination of parallel connections using two switches. In conclusion, this problem occurred becauseas we didn’t fully understand the structure of the breadboard.

    Final attempt with two switches

    Later we found out it was the issuematter of the direction of the current that made the LED lights not bright enough. Later we found out that the reason the LED lights weren’t bright enough was the issue of the direction of the current. We made two working switches with one battery (power source) by readjusting and organizing the resistors, wires, LED lights, and switches.

  7. Final Result

    8. The outcome includes two switches operated by positioning the coin, that lighten up the city.
